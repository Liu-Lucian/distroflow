Common Job Interview Formats

1. Phone Interviews

Phone interviews are often the first step in the hiring process. Typically lasting 15 to 30 minutes, these interviews allow employers to gauge your interest in the position and assess your qualifications.

Tip: Prepare a quiet space and have your resume handy. This allows you to refer to it easily if needed.

2. Video Interviews

Video interviews have surged in popularity, especially in recent years. They can be live (via platforms like Zoom or Skype) or pre-recorded. This format offers a more personal touch than a phone call while still allowing for flexibility.

Tip: Test your technology beforehand. Ensure your camera and microphone work well, and choose a professional background.

3. In-Person Interviews

In-person interviews are the traditional format and usually take place in the employer‚Äôs office. This setting provides an opportunity for both parties to interact face-to-face and gauge cultural fit.

Tip: Bring extra copies of your resume and be prepared with thoughtful questions about the role and company.
